Correlation between Payload and Success for KSC LC-39A

This scatter plot shows the correlation between payload mass and launch success outcomes for the KSC LC-39A launch site. The data points are color-coded based on the booster version category.

Key Elements:

  • Payload Range: The x-axis represents the payload mass in kilograms.
  • Success Class: The y-axis represents the success class (0 for failure and 1 for success).
  • Booster Versions: Different colors represent different booster version categories (v1.1, FT).

Observations:

  • The majority of the launches have payloads within the 2000-5000 kg range.
  • Successful launches (class 1) are scattered across various payload masses, indicating no strong correlation between payload mass and success rate.
  • Failures (class 0) are also spread out, with no significant clustering in any specific payload range.

Conclusion:

The scatter plot suggests that for the KSC LC-39A launch site, there is no clear correlation between payload mass and launch success. The success rate does not appear to be significantly influenced by the payload mass within the observed range.
